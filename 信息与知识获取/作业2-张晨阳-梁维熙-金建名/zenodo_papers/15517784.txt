Aging is more than just growing older&mdash;it&rsquo;s a&nbsp;<strong>biological process</strong>&nbsp;that impacts your body and mind. As you age, your cells face damage, and your&nbsp;<a title="The Science Behind Biology of Aging | ENTECH Magazine" href="https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GOVPUB-HE20-PURL-gpo46777/pdf/GOVPUB-HE20-PURL-gpo46777.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener nofollow">immune system</a>&nbsp;weakens. This increases the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and cancer. For instance, chronic inflammation, known as &ldquo;<strong>inflammaging</strong>,&rdquo; contributes to conditions such as atherosclerosis and tumors. However, there&rsquo;s hope. Advances in science are helping people live longer and healthier lives. These breakthroughs, from understanding the&nbsp;<strong>biology of aging</strong>&nbsp;to improving diets, are transforming&nbsp;<strong>health and longevity</strong>.
